Week Three

This week was centered around learning how to use Figma, so that we could visually produce ideas and create wireframes that would guide our coding process. I found Figma to be responsive and familiar but the lack of keyboard shortcuts and placement of tools in contrast to Adobe products was throwing me off slightly, along with hindering my work speed. I decided that in future when developing wireframes, I would swap to Adobe XD as I was more comfortable with it, along with the cloud saving keeping all my different projects in one area being a big bonus.

Week Four

We were at the beginnings of our first assignment this week, and Josh and Blaine set about the room to help students with personal problems regarding the assignment. My problem was that I’d shot to far ahead to early without considering the structural problems. I was getting confused about the appropriate number of pages and what they should address and so on until Josh explained to me that I should start with User Stories. User Stories turned out to be extremely helpful in planning my web layout and the contents it would contain. I was able to divulge the needs and wants of my theoretical target base, so with this knowledge in mind it was easier and faster to develop my wireframes as I now had a reference for structural guidance. We also experimented with CSS grid layouts and learned how to adapt the grid to certain needs.

Week Six

This week we digitally rendezvoused with our group members and discussed our proposed ideas for our new project. We decided on a website dedicated to finding homes for pets; an adoption base website much like the SPCA one. After this was confirmed we took to work building our user stories, as these would be vastly useful to constructing the theoretical idea of our website and how it would operate. We began listing questions we’d ask the SPCA representative we were aiming to meet with, along with listing pages the website would have and the corresponding information they’d contain. Having this all listed out would help us to better understand our user flows.

We met up in person and began discussing our various user flows and our expectations for the aesthetic of the website. We took a preliminary look at what aspect we could incorporate to set ourselves apart from our competitors. A key point was our venture in the construction of our block frames. We all created two iterations of each page for the website, leaving us with a variety of different options to analyze and amalgamate successfully. Once we had enough iterations of our block frames, we began discussing font and color elements to be involved in our style guide. We’re making slight progress on our wireframes, testing out our general idea pulled from the block frames, and making adjustments based on our ideal style guide as we go.

Reflection

The blog project started out as something tiresome and frustrating but as I grew to understand HTML and CSS somewhat better, the experience became more enjoyable. Trying again and again with no results until the refresh button would eventually yield some sort of change on the live server was a great feeling and pushed me to keep trying new things with my blog. The final outcome of my blog visually deviates from my Figma prototypes to an extent, which I put down to the vast distance between my coding skills and my design knowledge. Although I tried, I could never quite make my blog replicate the sleek design I had prototyped; with a lack of white space and extravagance in size for some elements being a key difference. This was alright though, because I was proud to have made something from scratch; and navigating through the pages brought me a sense of pride along with the knowledge I gained from the various setbacks.
